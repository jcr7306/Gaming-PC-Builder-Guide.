Learn How to Build Your Own Gaming PC

Building your own gaming PC is easier than most people think. This website explains the parts you need and the basic steps to get started.



Why Build Your Own PC?

  • It can save you money.
  • You get to choose the parts you want.
  • It is easier to upgrade later.
  • You can build a PC that fits your budget.

Parts You Need

  • CPU (Processor)
  • GPU (Graphics Card)
  • Motherboard
  • RAM (Memory)
  • Storage (SSD or HDD)
  • Power Supply (PSU)
  • Computer Case

Step-by-Step Guide to Building a Gaming PC

Step 1: Prepare Your Workspace

Find a clean table with plenty of room to work. Make sure you have a Phillips-head screwdriver and keep all of your PC parts nearby.

Step 2: Install the CPU

Lift the metal lever on the motherboard and carefully place the CPU into the socket. Make sure the triangle on the CPU lines up with the triangle on the motherboard before locking it into place.

Step 3: Install the RAM

Open the clips on the RAM slots. Line up the notch on the RAM stick and press down until it clicks into place.

Step 4: Install the SSD

If you are using an M.2 SSD, slide it into the slot on the motherboard and secure it with the small screw.

Step 5: Install the CPU Cooler

Apply thermal paste if needed, then attach the CPU cooler and connect the fan cable to the motherboard.

Step 6: Install the Motherboard

Place the motherboard inside the computer case and screw it into the standoffs.

Step 7: Install the Power Supply

Slide the power supply into the case and secure it with the four screws that came with it.

Step 8: Install the Graphics Card

Insert the graphics card into the top PCIe slot and secure it with screws. Connect the power cables if your graphics card requires them.

Step 9: Connect the Cables

Connect the motherboard power cable, CPU power cable, graphics card cables, storage cables, and the front panel connectors.

Step 10: Turn On the PC

Connect your monitor, keyboard, and mouse. Turn on the power supply and press the power button. If everything is connected correctly, the computer should start.

Step 11: Install Windows

Use a USB drive with Windows installed on it. Follow the setup instructions, then install drivers and updates.

Tips for First-Time Builders

  • Read the motherboard manual before you begin.
  • Keep your screws organized.
  • Do not force any parts into place.
  • Double-check all cable connections.
  • Take your time and be patient.
  • Watch a tutorial video if you get stuck.
